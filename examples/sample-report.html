First-principles read

The bottleneck is not only traffic. The core decision friction is buyer confidence: people want proof, comparison, and guidance before they commit.

What to avoid

Do not shift budget only toward the cheapest traffic. Lower-cost sessions may still underperform if they do not reduce trust friction.

Opportunity Map

Proof-led landing pages

Repeat comparison tables, validation snippets, and setup guidance where purchase anxiety is highest.

Search efficiency

Keep high-intent search but audit expensive terms and route them to stronger proof pages.

Creator reuse

Turn qualitative trust signals into reusable page modules, ads, and email content.

Email expansion

Use email as a confidence and repeat-purchase channel, not just a discount channel.

Risk and Rebuttal

Do not overclaim

  • Four weeks of data is directional, not definitive.
  • Creator content has low statistical confidence despite strong qualitative feedback.
  • Channel performance may be affected by offer timing, audience overlap, or landing page differences.

Validation needed

  • Run an A/B test for proof-led page modules.
  • Separate new customers from returning customers.
  • Compare channel performance by landing page type.

Action Roadmap

Week 1

Audit search terms and identify proof gaps on top landing pages.

Week 2

Launch proof-led page test with comparison, validation, and setup guidance.

Week 3

Repurpose creator trust signals into ads and email content.

Week 4

Review conversion quality by channel and decide budget shifts.
