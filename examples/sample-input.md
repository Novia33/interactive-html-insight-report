# Sample Input: Campaign Review Notes

This fictional input is intentionally generic. It shows the kind of messy source material the skill can turn into an interactive HTML report.

## Context

A small ecommerce team reviewed a recent acquisition campaign across search, social, email, and creator content. The goal was to understand which channels should receive more attention next month.

## Notes

- Paid search had the highest intent but also the highest cost per conversion.
- Social ads generated more traffic than expected, but visitors had lower purchase intent.
- Email drove fewer sessions but better repeat purchases.
- Creator content produced strong qualitative feedback, especially around product trust and setup confidence.
- Landing pages with comparison tables performed better than pages with only feature claims.
- Several customers mentioned uncertainty before purchase because they wanted proof, installation guidance, or third-party validation.
- The team has only four weeks of data, so conclusions should be treated as directional.

## Rough Metrics

| Channel | Sessions | Conversion Rate | Cost Index | Confidence |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Search | 12400 | 3.8% | 92 | Medium |
| Social | 21600 | 1.2% | 58 | Medium |
| Email | 5200 | 5.1% | 12 | High |
| Creator Content | 7800 | 2.4% | 34 | Low |

## Open Questions

- Should next month prioritize search efficiency or creator-led trust building?
- Which landing page elements should be repeated?
- Which findings are strong enough to act on, and which need validation?
